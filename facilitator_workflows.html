Wellness Wednesday

Facilitator workflow

Group walks, wellness discussions, recreational activities, and nutrition support. Mix of drop-in and pre-registered participants.

Promote & recruit
1
Weekly, before session
Post on social media + send direct messages
Share session details on the org's social media and send DMs to known participants. Include the registration QR code or clickable link so people can sign up in advance.
Social media post QR code → Session attendance log (Google Forms)
Before session
2
Day of, before start
Handle walk-ins — intake first
Any new participant who walks in without pre-registering must complete the Universal Intake + Consent Form in the database before joining. This captures demographics and the media release. No intake = no participation.
Database — Universal Intake Form
During session
3
Any point during the walk / activity
Record a short video clip of youth in action
Use your smartphone to capture a natural moment — youth on the walk, playing a game, sharing a moment. This doubles as your qualitative observation and social media content. Only film participants who have a signed media release in the database.
Smartphone camera Share to org social media account
After session
4
Same day, after session ends
Log attendance and observation notes in the database
Open the database and mark each participant as attended Wellness Wednesday. Add any brief observation notes — energy level, engagement, anything worth flagging. This is the official activity record.
Database — Activity log + notes
5
Within 24–48 hours after session
Send caregiver check-in via text or post
Send a follow-up text or social media post to caregivers with a link to the short Google Form check-in (2–3 questions on well-being and consistency). Keep it brief so they actually respond.
Text / DM Link → Caregiver check-in (Google Forms)

Create in the Park

Facilitator workflow

Open studio arts sessions with family participation, facilitated conversations, and periodic showcases. Mix of outreach and word-of-mouth recruitment.

Promote & recruit
1
Before each session
Promote via social media, flyers, and word of mouth
Share session details through whatever mix of outreach applies — social post, flyer, direct message. Include the registration link or QR code for advance sign-ups.
Social media / flyers / DM QR code → Session participation log (Google Forms)
Before session
2
Day of, before art activities begin
Complete intake for any new participants or walk-ins
New youth or family members need the Universal Intake + Consent Form in the database completed before they participate. The media release in the intake form is especially important here since artwork will be photographed.
Database — Universal Intake Form
During session
3
Throughout the session
Photograph completed artwork using the tagging protocol
When a youth finishes a piece, photograph it. Name the file or add a note with: date — session theme — youth initials (e.g., 2026-04-23_nature_JD). Upload to the shared Google Drive folder. The art is the data — no separate form needed.
Smartphone camera Google Drive — Create in the Park artwork folder
4
If families attend
Note family attendance in the participation log
When a caregiver or family member participates, mark them in the session participation log. This is a key data point for family engagement tracking.
Google Sheets — Session participation log
After session
5
Same day, after session ends
Complete brief facilitator mood and engagement observation
Fill out the short Google Form: overall mood in the room, any notable moments of connection or isolation, anything to flag. Should take under 3 minutes. Then log attendance and mark the activity in the database.
Google Forms — Facilitator mood observation Database — Activity log + notes
6
At program showcase milestone
Document the showcase
Photograph displayed work and note number of attendees. Write a short paragraph on what happened. Save to Google Drive. This is the culminating qualitative artifact for the cohort.
Smartphone camera Google Drive — Showcase documentation

Austin Youth Safety Program

Staff facilitator + youth-led workflow

Civic engagement cohort using photovoice, participatory research, and policy development. Staff facilitates most sessions; youth lead presentations.

Program start (once per cohort)
1
First session
Complete intake and administer pre-survey
Every youth participant completes the Universal Intake + Consent Form in the database. The media release covers photovoice images and published policy products. Immediately after, administer the civic knowledge pre-survey so you have a baseline before any learning happens.
Database — Universal Intake Form Google Forms — Civic knowledge pre-survey
Each session
2
Start of each session
Mark attendance and open database activity log
Log each participant as present in the database and note the session type (photovoice, dialogue, policy workshop, presentation). This is your ongoing participation record.
Database — Activity log
Photovoice sessions
3
During and after each photovoice session
Collect and archive youth photos + captions
Youth submit their photographs and written captions. Log each submission in the photovoice tracking sheet (date, youth initials, session theme). Upload images to the Google Drive archive folder. The photo and caption together are the qualitative artifact.
Google Sheets — Photovoice submission log Google Drive — Photovoice archive folder
Policy workshop sessions
4
At the end of each workshop
Save the policy product to the Drive archive
Whatever youth produce — a written brief, a slide deck, a set of recommendations — save it to the cohort folder in Google Drive. This is your evidence of policy analysis skill. No separate form needed; the document is the data.
Google Drive — Cohort policy products folder
Presentation to stakeholders
5
Immediately after each presentation
Complete the presentation observation log
Facilitator fills out the Google Form while details are fresh: youth confidence level, questions asked by stakeholders, any commitments or responses made by policymakers or community members. This is qualitative evidence that the advocacy had real-world reach.
Google Forms — Presentation observation + stakeholder response
Program end (once per cohort)
6
Final session
Administer post-survey and log in the database
Give youth the civic knowledge post-survey — same form as the pre-survey so responses can be compared. Then mark program completion for each participant in the database and add any final observation notes.
Google Forms — Civic knowledge post-survey Database — Program completion + notes

Mentoring

Mentor workflow (independent) + coordinator oversight

One-on-one and group mentoring. Mentors operate independently and complete their own data tasks. Coordinator monitors progress and reviews voice memos monthly.

Enrollment (coordinator task, once)
1
At enrollment
Coordinator completes intake in the database
Program coordinator (not the mentor) completes the Universal Intake + Consent Form for each youth participant in the database. Captures demographics, caregiver contact, and media release. Mentor receives the youth's name and profile once intake is done.
Database — Universal Intake Form
First 1–2 sessions (mentor task)
2
First or second meeting
Complete goal-setting form together with youth
Mentor and youth sit down together and fill out the goal-setting form. Mentor facilitates the conversation; youth provides the answers. Keep it conversational — this is a relationship-building moment, not a quiz.
Google Forms — Youth goal-setting form
After every session (mentor task)
3
Within a few hours of each session
Submit the session log
Mentor fills out the short session log Google Form: date, length, format, activities, attendance, engagement level, and any goals discussed. Should take under 5 minutes. This is the core ongoing data record for each youth.
Google Forms — Mentor session log
Monthly (mentor task)
4
Once a month
Record a 60-second voice memo
Open the voice memo app on your smartphone and record a 60-second reflection answering two questions: "What did I notice about this youth this month?" and "Any concerns or wins to flag?" Send the audio file to the coordinator via text or email. No written form required.
Smartphone voice memo app Text or email → coordinator
Program midpoint (youth + mentor task)
5
Halfway through program enrollment
Youth completes mid-point check-in
Mentor shares the Google Form link or QR code with the youth. Youth completes it independently. For younger participants, mentor can read questions aloud and record responses. Coordinator logs completion in the progress tracker.
Google Forms — Youth mid-point check-in Google Sheets — Program progress tracker (coordinator)
Program end (youth + coordinator task)
6
At or before final session
Youth completes exit survey; coordinator closes record
Youth completes the exit survey (same process as mid-point check-in). Coordinator marks program completion in the database, adds final notes, and updates the progress tracker with pre/post goal confidence scores from the goal-setting form and exit survey.
Google Forms — Youth exit survey Database — Program completion Google Sheets — Progress tracker

Safe Passage

Credible messenger workflow

Daily route presence with mediation, coaching, and referrals. Designed for field staff with smartphones. Exact shift structure to be confirmed with org leadership.

Enrollment (coordinator task, as needed)
1
When a youth receives direct services
Coordinator completes intake for youth receiving direct services
If a young person is connected to ongoing Safe Passage support (not just a one-time interaction), the coordinator completes the Universal Intake + Consent Form in the database. Walk-in or first-contact youth who receive a referral also need intake completed before being formally tracked.
Database — Universal Intake Form
Start of shift (daily)
2
Start of each shift
Log presence and route in the daily presence log
Mark your name, shift start time, and assigned route in the daily presence log. This establishes that presence happened and where.
Google Sheets — Daily presence log
⚑ Shift check-in/check-out structure needs confirmation with org leadership before finalizing this step.
During shift — significant interaction
3
Immediately after a notable interaction
Record a 30-second field voice memo
After a mediation, a youth in distress, or a safety concern — open your voice memo app and record a quick 30-second note: what happened, what you did, any follow-up needed. Send to coordinator via text or a shared channel. Much faster than typing in the field.
Smartphone voice memo app Text or shared channel → coordinator
During shift — incident
4
As incidents occur
Submit incident documentation form
For formal incidents (violence, de-escalation required, police contact), complete the incident documentation Google Form as soon as it is safe to do so. This is the official record. The voice memo is supplemental context.
Google Forms — Incident documentation
During shift — referral
5
When a referral is made
Log the referral
When you connect a youth to a resource or partner organization, log it in the referral tracking sheet: youth initials, date, what they were referred for, and to which partner. Coordinator follows up to confirm the referral was completed.
Google Sheets — Referral tracking log
Weekly (coordinator task)
6
End of each week
Coordinator updates hotspot tracker and reviews voice memos
Coordinator listens to the week's voice memos, logs key themes and patterns in the hotspot tracker, and notes any safety concerns by location. Updates database activity records for youth who received direct services that week.
Google Sheets — Hotspot trend tracker Database — Activity and notes update
Quarterly (coordinator task)
7
Once per quarter
Send perceived safety survey to youth and families
Coordinator sends the short Google Form perceived safety survey via text or DM to youth and families connected to the program. Results feed into outcome reporting.
Text / DM Link → Perceived safety survey (Google Forms)

Youth Solidarity Program

Staff + youth-led workflow

Community organizing and advocacy through outreach trips, restorative circles, collection drives, and public advocacy. Staff facilitates some sessions; youth lead others.

Enrollment (once per participant)
1
At enrollment
Complete intake in the database
Staff completes the Universal Intake + Consent Form for each youth in the database. The media release here covers outreach trip photos, social media posts featuring youth, and any published advocacy materials. Must be done before any photos are taken or content is published.
Database — Universal Intake Form
Promote & recruit
2
Before each activity
Promote via social media, flyers, word of mouth, and outreach partners
Use whichever mix of channels fits the activity — social post, partner referrals, direct outreach. Include registration link or QR code where advance sign-up applies.
Social media / flyers / DM / partner referral
Each session — all activity types
3
Start of each session
Log attendance and activity type in the database
Mark each participant present and note the activity type (restorative circle, outreach trip, collection drive, advocacy activity). This is the foundation of your participation record.
Database — Activity log
Restorative circles
4
During and immediately after each circle
Designated note-taker captures 2–3 themes or quotes
Before the circle begins, assign a note-taker (rotating role — youth or staff). After the circle, the note-taker writes 2–3 key themes, memorable quotes, or moments of shift in a shared Google Doc. No individual attribution. Save to the Drive archive.
Google Docs — Circle notes (saved to Drive archive)
Outreach trips
5
During each outreach trip
Photograph youth in action + log items distributed
Take photos during the trip (consent must be in the database). After the trip, log the number of participants, items distributed, and location in the outreach activity log. Photos go to Google Drive and can be shared to social media.
Smartphone camera → Google Drive + social media Google Sheets — Outreach activity log
Advocacy activities (presentations, petitions, posts)
6
As each advocacy output is completed
Log the advocacy output and note reach or response
When youth complete an advocacy action — a presentation, petition, social media post, or public speaking moment — log it in the advocacy output sheet: date, type of action, estimated reach or audience, and any notable response from partners or community.
Google Sheets — Advocacy output log
Periodically (after major activities)
7
After significant events or milestones
Send youth reflection form + partner feedback form
After a major outreach trip, presentation, or organizing milestone, send youth the short reflection Google Form (empathy and systems understanding). Separately, send the partner feedback form to any partner organizations involved in the activity.
Google Forms — Youth reflection form Google Forms — Partner feedback form
